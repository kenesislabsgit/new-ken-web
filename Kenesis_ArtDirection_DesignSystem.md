## 02 — DESIGN PRINCIPLES
## Six Foundational Rules
These principles govern every design decision — from landing page to dashboard UI.
## 03 — COLOR SYSTEM
## The Kenesis Palette
Two modes: Dark (operations center, dashboard) and Light (marketing, reports). Orange is the
single brand accent in both. Status colors are shared across both modes and are never
repurposed.
## BRAND & NEUTRAL
## STATUS (ISA-101)
## PRINCIPLE 01
## Depth Before Decoration
Use atmospheric layering —
gradients, glows, blur — to
create physical depth. No
decorative patterns or
illustrations. Depth should feel
real, not designed.
## PRINCIPLE 02
## Glass Floats, Steel
## Grounds
Glassmorphic elements (nav,
cards, overlays) float above the
dark base. Skeuomorphic
controls (buttons, toggles) feel
physical and tactile. Never mix
the two on the same layer.
## PRINCIPLE 03
## One Accent. One Glow.
Orange (#FF6B35) is the only
light source in the dark. It glows.
Everything else is dark gray or
white. No secondary accent
colors in UI. Status colors
(green/amber/red) are
functional, not aesthetic.
## PRINCIPLE 04
Type Does the Work
Headlines are large and heavy
— they carry the visual weight,
not icons or illustrations.
Monospace for all data,
technical values, and system
output. No decorative fonts.
## PRINCIPLE 05
## Shadows Are Purposeful
Shadows encode hierarchy —
flat = inactive, soft shadow =
elevated, strong shadow =
modal. Glow shadows (orange)
indicate active/interactive states.
Never decorative.
## PRINCIPLE 06
## Industrial Precision
8px grid, always. Monospace for
technical data. HUD corners on
camera feeds. Terminal-style
logs. Every UI element should
feel like it belongs in a control
room.
